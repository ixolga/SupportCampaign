What can you do?

Beeswax

Beeswax wrap is a sustainable alternative for plastic wraps. It is washable, reusable, and compostable. The food wrapped in it lasts depending on the food itself, but it can be refrigerated for up to three weeks. However, beeswax wrap is not recommended to use with raw meat. It can be DIY-ed as it is simple to make. You will only need cotton fabric, organic jojoba oil, and tree resin. there are many ways on how to make one by searching online. Click here for an in-depth guide.


Bamboo

Bamboo is a fast-growing renewable resource that can replace plastic items such as drinking straws tableware. Not only it reduces the use of plastic and has reduced production costs, but it is also lightweight, durable and compostable.


Cassava Bag as Mailer Packaging

Cassava Bag are 100% compostable and biodegrades in 105 days. The bags are made from cassava starch and other natural materials. These are soluble in hot water and are not harmful to living things in the event of consumption. Makes use of eco-friendly soy-based ink and these can be reused up to 4-5 times.

This is the sensible option compared to traditional plastics made from non-renewable resources and other plastics claiming to have “degradable” features. The cassava bags are perfect for mailing big items and can be used as trash or laundry bags as well.



Reusable Grocery Bag

Reusable Grocery Bag is an alternative to single use paper or plastic bags. It is often a tote bag made from fabric such as canvas, natural fibers such as Jute, woven synthetic fibers, or a thick plastic that is more durable than disposable plastic bags, allowing multiple use. It is also easier to hold.
